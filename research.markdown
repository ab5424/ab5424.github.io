My research focuses on the chemistry and physics of solid-state materials, with an emphasis on atomistic mechanisms that control transport and thermodynamic behavior.

## Research themes

### Ion and defect transport in solids
I study how point defects, local structure, and composition influence ionic and electronic transport in crystalline and disordered materials. A central goal is to identify physically interpretable descriptors that connect chemistry to mobility.

### Structure-property relationships
I investigate how local and long-range structure control measurable properties such as conductivity, stability, and thermal response. This includes comparing trends across material families to derive general design principles.

### Materials for energy applications
My work targets materials relevant to electrochemical and energy technologies, including solid-state ionic conductors and related functional compounds used in energy storage and conversion.

## Methods

I use computational approaches spanning first-principles calculations, thermodynamic analysis, and data-driven interpretation of simulation results. These methods are combined to move from microscopic mechanisms to experimentally testable predictions.
